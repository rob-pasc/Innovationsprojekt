# Gamified Learning - Media Literacy Training
## Project Summary
The Media Literacy Training Platform is an interactive, browser-based educational tool designed to combat the growing difficulty of identifying manipulated, AI-generated content, and phishing attempts. Transitioning from a purely technical detection tool to a pedagogical solution, the platform focuses on "Help for Self-Help" by empowering users to recognize fakes independently through a "Hoxhunt for Media Literacy" approach.

The software addresses critical deficits in current security awareness training, which is often perceived as monotonous, overly theoretical, and lacking real-world application. By integrating gamification - including narrative story modes, point systems and immediate feedback - the platform increases user engagement and ensures a sustainable strengthening of digital media and security competence.

## Goals
- **Enhance Media Competence**: Transition users from passive consumers to active analysts capable of identifying AI-generated media and phishing risks in real-world scenarios.

- **Maximize Engagement via Gamification**: Use interactive game mechanics (points, progress tracking, and narrative "Detective Modes") to overcome the boredom associated with traditional training.

- **Low-Barrier Accessibility**: Provide a completely browser-based (SaaS) experience to eliminate installation hurdles and ensure compatibility with strict corporate IT security policies.

- **Immediate Pedagogical Feedback**: Deliver instant, understandable explanations at the moment a mistake is made, explaining why a choice was incorrect and what patterns were missed.

- **Privacy-First Design**: Build trust by offering a solution that does not require access to sensitive internal data or personal emails, positioning it as a GDPR-compliant alternative to premium AI-based training tools.

- **Sustained Learning Connection**: Implement psychological principles like loss aversion (similar to Duolingo) to encourage regular training and long-term retention of media literacy skills.
